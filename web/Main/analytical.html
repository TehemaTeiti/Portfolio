Analysis of skills

During this training, I made several projects which were instructives and where I could put in practice what I have learned, resolve problems I was confronted with and discover more about the IoT domain. Coming from a computer science domain, I discover the electronical and signal processing fields. That helps to understand the process of an IoT device, from the design of a circuit board to the deployement of web services. The following criteria are used to determine the level:

  • AP- level of application: follow-up of instructions or procedures
  • AN- level of analysis: improvement or optimization of solutions or proposals
  • M - level of proficiency: program design or specification definitions
  • EX - level of expertise: definition of orientations or strategies
Also, the mode field determines the main acquisition methods for an activity:
  • IT: Initial Training
  • PE: Peer Exchange
  • ST: Self-Training
  • PP: Professional Practice

Also, the manufacture of a nanoparticles sensor was very interesting. I could see at a chemical level how a sensor can detect specific gases and understand the problems around measurements depending on the environment (humidity, temperature, …).

Self evaluation

Thanks to this training, I learn a lot on the IoT domain in term of electronic, software and network infrastructure.

I mainly enhance my computer science skills through Middle and Service training unit through courses and projects. This concept was a discovery for me and I very appreciate developing distributed applications and learn new communication protocols. Moreover, these experiences reinforce me about being able to choose the right technologies and architecture to use in IoT systems regarding needs and applications.

Also, I am able to understand electronic schematics, to design them and to acquire data from a sensor through a microcontroller like Arduino. I understand how sensors can communicate through wireless networks to an end-point application and what issues are involved in the communication process.

Another point that I enjoyed during this training was group projects that we made all along, especially the innovative project. We had management and social psychology courses that help us to organize our work and to understand why conflicts may happen in a team and how we can solve them.

However, skills are still missing at the end of this journey. I have a lack of knowledge in electronic and signal processing, so it was difficult for me to understand related courses in detail. Despite everything, I had an overview of these fields and I understand the issues involving. In this way, it will be much easier to understand if a problem occurs and in which way I have to go to solve it.

Balance sheet

Building this portfolio allowed me to take a step back about this year. On one hand, I could highlight skills that I gained and see what I am able to do in the IoT domain. On the other hand, I could see what skills are still missing and if I want to develop a complete IoT application, I will probably need to work on them.
